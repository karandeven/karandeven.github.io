How I Learn

Learning Philosophy

My approach to learning is guided by principles from Self-Determination Theory, developed by psychologists Edward Deci and Richard Ryan. This framework emphasizes three core psychological needs that drive sustained motivation and growth.

Autonomy means choosing what to learn based on genuine interest rather than external pressure. I select projects that align with real-world problems I want to solve, which keeps the learning process meaningful.

Competence comes from building working systems and seeing tangible results. Each successful deployment or automation script reinforces understanding and builds confidence to tackle more complex challenges.

Relatedness involves connecting with the broader engineering community through shared tools, practices, and open-source contributions. Learning becomes more effective when grounded in real contexts where others face similar problems.

This approach keeps technical learning sustainable over the long term. Rather than chasing trends or certifications, I focus on building genuine capability through projects that demand actual understanding.
